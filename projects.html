"I AM A MAN" Infographic

PROCESS

For this infographic, I began with gathering my research and deciding out how I would like to communicate these pivotal parts of history. I utilized the knowledge I received when I was a participant in the Civil Rights Pilgrimage with UW- Eau Claire. It surrounded a worker’s rights protest in Memphis Tennessee in 1968. After gathering all this information, I moved into creating a moodboard to define my ideal color palette, font and layout. After gathering those ideas, I combined both parts of my research and began to design. I did a combination of sketching, test prototypes and digital design to create this piece in whole. The goal was to make it interactive, visually appealing and readable. I am very proud with the outcome of this project.

Calder Poster

PROCESS

This poster was based on a project that was centralized on the exploration of letters and testing the boundaries of how readable it has to be. I researched who Alexander Calder was and the works he has produced. I found that he creates very geometric, limited primary color palette sculptural pieces. It gave me inspiration in how I decided to design these pieces. I explored in the atmosphere of how to take advantage of the viewer’s line of sight but also bending the rules of how far I can go before it makes no sense for the viewer. I did a combination of sketching and digital design.

Alternative Micron Sustainable Packaging

PROCESSS

This was an opportunity to dive into the world of sustainable packaging and how to make it enticing. I decided to do it on Micron Pens due to the amount of plastic that is used within the packaging. My goals for the project was to be in a container that can be reused and utilize the packaging to communicate the benefits and quality of the pens.
